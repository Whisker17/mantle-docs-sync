---
title: "Migration of Data Availability to Ethereum Blobs"
---

Mantle Network is initiating a strategic migration of its Data Availability (DA) layer from EigenDA to Ethereum Blobs (EIP-4844). This transition marks a significant milestone in our technical roadmap, effectively transitioning Mantle from a Validium architecture to a standard ZK Rollup.

### Key Drivers for Migration

#### 1. Security Upgrade: From Validium to True Rollup

The most critical driver for this migration is the qualitative leap in security.

* **Current State (EigenDA):** Under L2Beat’s framework, Mantle is currently classified as “Others”. Security relies on the honesty of external EigenDA operators. While robust, this introduces a trusted dependency outside of Ethereum.
* **Future State (Blobs):** By posting data directly to Ethereum Blobs, Mantle inherits the full cryptoeconomic security of Ethereum. As long as Ethereum’s consensus holds, Mantle’s data is guaranteed to be available. This closes the security loop: ZK proofs verify state correctness, and Blobs guarantee data transparency.

#### 2. Economic Predictability & Stability

With the successful completion of the Fusaka upgrade and the implementation of BPO2, Ethereum is progressively realizing its vision for industrial-scale data availability.The current network parameters—Target=14 and Max=21 blobs per block—represent a massive leap in throughput capacity compared to the Pectra era (Target=6, Max=9).

This dramatic expansion in supply has fundamentally reshaped the fee market. We have observed that Blob pricing currently remains at a highly competitive baseline, creating a perfect window for migration. This environment allows Mantle to secure the uncompromised safety of Layer 1 without the historical cost penalty, offering a cost structure that is both predictable and sustainable for our high-performance network.

#### 3. Engineering Alignment with OP Stack

Migrating to Blobs allows Mantle to align more closely with the upstream OP Stack.

* **Reduced Complexity:** Maintaining a custom integration for EigenDA required extensive modifications to the `op-batcher` and `op-node`.
* **Native Compatibility:** Switching to Blobs enables us to utilize the native data submission logic of the OP Stack. This reduces technical debt, accelerates our ability to merge upstream updates, and minimizes the risk of bugs derived from custom adaptations.

### What This Means for the Ecosystem

#### For Users

* No action required
* Improved withdrawal safety under Ethereum-native DA guarantees

#### For Developers & Infrastructure Providers

* No API or RPC changes
* Improved compatibility with Ethereum tooling and rollup standards

#### For Mantle Network

* Classification as a **standard ZK Rollup**
* Stronger alignment with L2Beat rollup security criteria
* Clear path toward **Stage 1 decentralization milestones**

***

This migration reflects our commitment to building the most secure and scalable financial infrastructure. By combining Ethereum-native DA with OP Succinct’s ZK proving system, Mantle continues to advance toward a secure, scalable, and Ethereum-aligned rollup architecture.
